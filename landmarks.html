Discover Azerbaijan's Landmarks

List of Landmarks

The Scenery of Baku

Baku’s landscape beautifully merges modern architecture with natural scenery. The iconic Flame Towers dominate the skyline, reflecting off the calm waters of the Caspian Sea. Alongside these futuristic structures, the city’s historic charm and scenic waterfront promenade create a captivating blend of old and new.

Read more about the Flame Towers on Wikipedia.

Old City (Icherisheher)

Baku’s Old City, or Icherisheher, is the historic heart of Azerbaijan’s capital and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Surrounded by ancient stone walls, it features narrow alleys, centuries-old mosques, and iconic landmarks like the Maiden Tower and the Palace of the Shirvanshahs. The Old City offers a unique blend of medieval architecture and vibrant local culture, all set against the backdrop of Baku’s modern skyline.

Learn more about the Old City of Baku on UNESCO.

Gobustan Rock Carvings

The Gobustan rock carvings, located in Gobustan National Park, Azerbaijan, are ancient petroglyphs dating back over 40,000 years. These fascinating engravings depict scenes of daily life, including hunting, dancing, and rituals, offering a glimpse into the lives of prehistoric communities. Recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, Gobustan stands as a significant testament to humanity’s early creativity and cultural expression.

Read more about Gobustan National Park on Wikipedia.

Sheki Khan’s Palace

Sheki Khan’s Palace, built in 1762, is a stunning example of Azerbaijani architecture and craftsmanship. Located in the historic town of Sheki, the palace was the summer residence of the Sheki Khans. It’s renowned for its intricate stained glass windows (shebeke), vibrant frescoes, and ornate wooden carvings. Surrounded by lush gardens, the palace reflects the rich cultural heritage and artistic traditions of the region.

Learn more about the Sheki Khan’s Palace on Wikipedia.

Tufandag Mountain

Gabala, often called the "Switzerland of Azerbaijan," is home to the stunning Tufandag Mountain Resort. Surrounded by the majestic Caucasus Mountains, Gabala offers breathtaking snow-capped peaks perfect for skiing and snowboarding in winter, as well as hiking and nature walks in summer. The area combines natural beauty with modern amenities, making it a top destination for adventure seekers and nature lovers alike.

Explore more about Tufandag on Wikipedia.
